Multilingual Entity Insertion in Wikipedia

Formalising a new information-retrieval task — where to insert a link to a given entity in a text — and solving it across 105 languages with a small encoder that nearly doubles GPT-4.

The problem

Wikipedia's value comes in large part from its dense web of internal links, but adding them is slow, manual work — and the gap is worst in under-resourced languages. We reframed this as entity insertion: given a target entity and a source article, find the exact span of text where a link to that entity should be placed. It's a retrieval problem, but one that has to reason about local context rather than whole-document relevance.

A 958M-link, 105-language benchmark

Working with the Wikimedia Foundation, I built the first large-scale multilingual benchmark for the task by scraping the full Wikipedia and engineering a deduplication and filtering pipeline that turns raw dumps into clean (context, target) training pairs. The result spans 958M links across 105 languages, from the highest-resourced down to the long tail — and is fully open-sourced.

xLocEI — a small model that beats GPT-4

Rather than prompt a large LLM, I fine-tuned an XLM-RoBERTa-base encoder (xLocEI) directly on the benchmark. Evaluated macro-averaged over 20 languages, it nearly doubles GPT-4's hits@1 at a small fraction of the inference cost, and it generalises zero-shot to languages unseen during training.

Classical baselines (BM25 lexical retrieval and dense retrieval) were also benchmarked to quantify the gap between traditional IR and the learning-based approach.
